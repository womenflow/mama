Being pregnant is a full-time job. Being pregnant in summer? That's overtime — swollen ankles, endless heat, and a body that's already working hard enough, thanks.

Gentle movement genuinely helps: it eases swelling, keeps circulation happy, and can lift your mood on the sticky days. Here are seven safe, cooling moves for your summer bump. **As always, check with your provider first — every pregnancy is its own story.**

## First, the Golden Rules
Before anything, three things to keep in mind all summer:
- **Hydrate more than feels necessary.** Water is your best friend now.
- **Move in the cool hours** — early morning or evening, never peak heat.
- **Listen to your body.** Dizzy, breathless, or just done? Stop. No exercise is worth pushing past that.

## 1. Ankle Circles (The Swelling Fighter)
Sit comfortably, lift one foot, and draw slow circles with your ankle. Simple, but it gets stubborn fluid moving. Do it while you watch TV — 10 circles each direction.

## 2. Seated Marching
Sit tall and gently lift one knee, then the other, like a slow-motion march. Keeps blood flowing without any impact or overheating.

## 3. Gentle Cat-Cow
On hands and knees, softly arch and round your back. It eases that lower-back ache your bump is negotiating with, and it feels *wonderful*.

## 4. Wall Push-Ups
Hands on a wall, lean in and press back out. Keeps your upper body strong for all the carrying ahead — baby included.

![Pregnant woman walking gently in a shaded park](/images/mama-s2.png)

## 5. Side-Lying Leg Lifts
Lie on your side and lift the top leg slowly. Great for hips and glutes, which will thank you during labor and beyond.

## 6. A Shaded Walk
Never underestimate a slow stroll in the shade or a cool mall. **Ten to fifteen minutes** keeps circulation moving and your mind calmer. Bring water. Always bring water.

## 7. Deep Breathing + Gentle Stretch
End by sitting tall, reaching your arms overhead, and taking five slow, deep breaths. Practice now and you'll have it ready for the big day.

## You're Doing Great
Summer pregnancy is no small thing, and showing up for these gentle moves — even some days, not all — is a win. **Go slow, stay cool, drink the water, and be kind to the amazing body doing all this work.**
